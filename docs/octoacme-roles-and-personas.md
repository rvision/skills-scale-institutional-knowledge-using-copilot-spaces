# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure that user needs and usability standards are factored into features and flows from inception to delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct or review user research and feedback
- Collaborate on design mockups and prototypes
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met
- Coordinate usability testing with stakeholders
- Provide design assets and specifications to development teams

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce rework caused by usability issues discovered late in development
- Ensure user needs drive feature decisions

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Collaboration tools (e.g., Figma, Miro) for design handoffs
- Usability testing reports shared with Product Managers and stakeholders

### Interaction with Existing Roles
Work closely with Product Managers on user stories and acceptance criteria; consult Developers during implementation for feasibility; provide feedback cycles to stakeholders; align with Project Managers on design milestone timelines.

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers bridge development and operations to streamline builds, deployments, and system reliability across the software delivery lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Automate CI/CD pipelines and deployment processes
- Manage infrastructure as code (IaC)
- Set up monitoring, alerting, and incident response runbooks
- Advise on cloud architecture and security best practices
- Maintain environment consistency across dev, staging, and production

### Goals
- Minimize deployment risk and downtime
- Improve release frequency and reliability
- Enable developers to ship faster with confidence

### Typical Communication
- Deployment schedules and release coordination with Project Managers
- Incident response channels and post-mortems
- Infrastructure documentation and runbooks

### Interaction with Existing Roles
Support Developers and QA in test and release processes; partner with Project Managers for deployment scheduling; collaborate with Security Champions for risk and compliance; coordinate with Product Managers on release timelines.

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business needs into clear, actionable requirements, bridging the gap between stakeholders and the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and validate solution requirements
- Map current and future-state processes to identify improvement opportunities
- Validate acceptance criteria with business stakeholders
- Maintain traceability between business goals and delivered features
- Support prioritization by quantifying business impact

### Goals
- Ensure delivered solutions address real business needs
- Reduce ambiguity and rework caused by unclear requirements
- Support data-driven decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Business requirements documents (BRDs) and user story refinement sessions
- Regular check-ins with Product Managers and Project Managers

### Interaction with Existing Roles
Serve as a communication bridge between stakeholders, Product Managers, and Developers; validate that deliverables address business goals; assist Project Managers with scope clarity; support Developers in understanding complex business rules.

---

## Security Champions

### Role Summary
Security Champions advocate for secure development practices and risk mitigation in all phases of the project lifecycle, ensuring security is built in rather than bolted on.

### Responsibilities
- Advise on threats, security controls, privacy requirements, and compliance
- Lead threat modeling and security design reviews
- Guide response to reported vulnerabilities
- Promote security awareness and best practices within the team
- Review PRs and architecture decisions for security implications

### Goals
- Reduce security vulnerabilities introduced during development
- Ensure compliance with relevant standards and regulations
- Foster a security-first engineering culture

### Typical Communication
- Security review sessions during planning and design phases
- Vulnerability reports and remediation tracking
- Security incident coordination with DevOps and Project Managers

### Interaction with Existing Roles
Advise Developers, QA, Product Managers, and Project Managers during feature development and release; coordinate with DevOps Engineers for secure infrastructure; escalate critical risks to Project Managers and sponsors per the risk escalation path.

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- The expanded set of personas—including UX Designers, DevOps Engineers, Business Analysts, and Security Champions—enables more realistic cross-functional scenarios and helps teams practice collaboration across all key roles in a modern software delivery process.
